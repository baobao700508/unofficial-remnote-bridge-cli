# Switching from Anki to RemNote

Coming from Anki? We got you! Here's an overview of how you can get used to RemNote and where you can find Anki's features.

# Adjust Your Note-Taking Method in 3 Steps

Here are the most basic differences between making flashcards in RemNote and in Anki. In RemNote:

1.  **Write flashcards while taking notes.** Make flashcards directly in your notes, while in class or studying. Just type `Question == Answer` in any bullet (Rem).
    
2.  **Organize notes documents with folders.** Use [folders and documents](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6030703-documents-and-folders) to organize your notes. For most students, we recommend creating a folder for each class, with a document for each lecture or reading session.
    
3.  **Write short, organized cards by using indented bullets.** Save typing and make your cards faster to read and review! Press _Tab_ to [indent](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/8196578-outlines-and-terminology). All higher-level bullets appear on each card, giving you a concise view of the context of each question.

# The Best of Anki, Upgraded

RemNote has Anki's best features and plugins built in. Here's how we've made them even better.

## 🗂 Cards

**✅ Make Cards in Your Notes** (no more messy card table)

Place cards right next to normal text notes. Make cards anywhere by typing `Question == Answer`.

**✅ Short Cards With Indentation** (no more wordy questions)

Indent bullets by pressing _Tab_. Make quick flashcards by including shared context in a parent bullet.

**✅** **Built-In [Image Occlusion](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6511625-image-occlusion-cards)** (easy, simple selection)

Hover over any image and click the image occlusion button. Built-in AI text label selection, labels, links, sequence cards, and more.

[![](https://downloads.intercomcdn.com/i/o/898739737/cabb3838ee1db3a975bd776e/image.png?expires=1772827200&signature=826faf78c39b7600e4504b54957fa95e5789376158d1e02f89232835f353cbbe&req=fCkvEcp3moJYFb4f3HP0gAuMFwRA5ehVHYwc1uYEZgPlAmiQGPn6MrIdk%2FzU%0Au3txJQyixzaWslJtIg%3D%3D%0A)](https://downloads.intercomcdn.com/i/o/898739737/cabb3838ee1db3a975bd776e/image.png?expires=1772827200&signature=826faf78c39b7600e4504b54957fa95e5789376158d1e02f89232835f353cbbe&req=fCkvEcp3moJYFb4f3HP0gAuMFwRA5ehVHYwc1uYEZgPlAmiQGPn6MrIdk%2FzU%0Au3txJQyixzaWslJtIg%3D%3D%0A)

**✅** **Make Structured Cards With [Tables](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/8118336-tables)** (no more boilerplate card templates)

Type `/table` to add a table. Each row and column of a table can generate a flashcard.

**✅** **Make [Cloze Deletions](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6025481-creating-flashcards#h_80f8c0a794) Anywhere With `{{`** (no more switching note types).

Make clozes in any bullet by typing {{ . Other types of cards can have clozes inside of them for extra practice.

**✅** **Equivalent Note Types**

The following types of cards replace Anki's default note types:

-   **[Basic (single-line) card](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6025481-creating-flashcards#h_594d2e06f8)** (equivalent to the _Basic_ note type in Anki): Type `Question == Answer` anywhere. For a bidirectional card (equivalent to the _Basic and reversed card_ note type in Anki), type `Question <> Answer`.
    
-   **[Cloze card](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6025481-creating-flashcards#h_80f8c0a794)** (equivalent to the _Cloze_ note type in Anki): Type `{{` to start an occluded (hidden) section, and `}}` to end it, or highlight some existing text and type `{` to occlude it.
    
-   **[Image occlusion card](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6511625-image-occlusion-cards)** (equivalent to the Image Occlusion plugin in Anki): Hover over any image and press the flashcards button in the top-right.
    

RemNote also needs some new types of cards to account for being outline-based:

-   **[Multi-line card](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/9216774-multi-line-list-set-flashcards):** Type `Question ===` to start an answer that has multiple items on different lines. Press Enter between each line.
    
-   **[List-Answer card](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/9216774-multi-line-list-set-flashcards#h_6171b40ac8):** Type `Question ==1.` These are like multi-line cards, but only one item is shown at a time, and all items are scheduled together.
    

You can use [tables](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/8118336-tables) to create flashcards from structured information, like you might use custom note types in Anki.

## 📄 Organization

**✅ Organize Cards with Documents & Folders** (no more sorting into decks)

In RemNote, since flashcards get created right within your notes, there are no decks – instead there are just [folders and documents](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6030703-documents-and-folders), and you can study from any folder, document, or combination thereof.

Click _Create_ in the left sidebar to make a folder for your class or topic of study. Add documents to the folder for each lecture or chapter.

**✅ Beautiful [Flashcard Home](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/7925835-the-flashcard-home)** (no more cluttered deck list)

View all documents with flashcards from the _Flashcards_ item on the sidebar.

**✅ Built-In [Document Prioritization](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/7950982-setting-priorities-and-disabling-flashcards)** (no more practicing cards you don't care about)

Practice the cards you care most about first. Click the down arrow next to the _Practice_ button in the Flashcards Home and select _Active, Maintaining_, or _Paused_ from the _Priority_ drop-down.

## 🧠 Practice

**✅ Global Queue**

Quickly practice all the cards due for review. Just click the flashcards number to the right of _Flashcards_ in the sidebar.

**✅ [Custom Schedulers](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6958056-custom-schedulers)**

Click your name in the top left, then _Settings_, then _Schedulers_.

**✅ [Statistics](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/7970392-flashcard-statistics)**

Go to _Flashcards_ in the left sidebar, then choose the _Statistics_ tab.

## 🚀 Everything Else!

**✅ [Shared Documents](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6030805-sharing-rems) & Flashcards**

Click _Share_ in the top right to share your documents and cards with your friends in two clicks! Choose _Shared Flashcards_ in the left sidebar to find documents others have shared.

Unlike in Anki, you can also conveniently [collaborate on notes and flashcards with other people](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/7322908-how-can-i-create-flashcards-with-someone-else).

**✅ Plugins & Themes**

Click your name in the top left, then click _Plugins_.

**✅ Desktop App**

For fully offline access and improved performance, download the desktop app at [remnote.com/download](https://www.remnote.com/download).

**✅ Mobile App**

To practice flashcards and jot down notes on the go, download the free mobile app. Find it in your device's app store or visit [remnote.com/mobile](https://www.remnote.com/mobile).

# Importing Existing Cards from Anki

In many cases, we recommend just getting started with RemNote and not worrying about your Anki content – for instance, if you're starting a new semester and don't urgently need to practice the material from last semester, it may be easier to start with a clean slate.

However, if you eventually want to start shifting over to RemNote full-time, or if you have content you urgently need to continue studying right now and you don't want to be studying in two apps at once, you can import flashcards from Anki. See [Importing from Anki](https://help.remnote.com/en/articles/6751471-importing-from-anki) for complete details. If you're used to this kind of thing and want the cheat sheet, here it is: In Anki, export the deck(s) you want to move as an apkg (_File > Export_), checking all four checkboxes (from _Include scheduling information_ to _Support older Anki versions_). Then, in RemNote, choose _Settings > Account > Import_, choose _Anki_, and select the apkg file.

Card schedules are preserved when importing from Anki. Some parts of your cards will not look exactly the same, depending on what kind of formatting features were used, but most types of cards will work great.
